Our story

Give nurses back their time.

During the pandemic, we watched nurses run in and out of rooms to answer questions a tablet could answer faster. We built Dispatch to fix that.

During the pandemic, I saw people working in hospital settings and something became painfully clear: nurses were constantly running in and out of patient rooms just to find out what someone needed. A glass of water. A blanket. Pain medication. Each interruption pulled them away from critical care; and patients waited longer for everything.

That frustration sparked an idea: what if patients could tell us exactly what they needed before anyone entered the room? Today, Dispatch serves hospitals across the Pacific Northwest, helping care teams respond faster and giving nurses back hours every shift to focus on what matters most — caring for patients.

Mission

Give nurses back their time.

Every minute a nurse spends figuring out what a patient needs is a minute they’re not providing care. We build technology that puts patients and care teams on the same page before anyone enters the room. Clear requests. Faster responses. Better outcomes for everyone.

Vision

Healthcare that’s transparent.

We see a future where no patient waits wondering if their call was heard, and no nurse walks into a room unprepared. A future where technology handles the coordination so humans can focus on healing. That’s the standard we’re building toward.
